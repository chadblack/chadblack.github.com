Structure vs Agency

Posted: Sat, 05 Feb 2011

Structure vs Agency. The journal article on structure and agency was interesting and reminded me of a debate that helped me understand the concepts. As the author indicated trying to understand structure can be difficult, but when an example is applied it becomes much clearer. The debate focused around the amount of structure and agency within the lives of African American slaves in the south. A historian by the name of Eugene Genovese argued in his book Roll, Jordan, Roll: The World the Slaves Made (1974), that slaves in the South possessed agency within their lives (or some amount of freedom of choice) as evidenced by slave culture and religion. He argued that even when families were broken up other males would step in to fill the role of the protector. Wilma Dunaway wrote that very little agency existed in the life of slaves because slavery in itself was the equivalent of “social death.” I thought Dunaway won the debate when she pointed that Genovese’s agency argument was based on the model of the large plantation with lots of slaves, when in reality the majority of slaves were held on small farms in small numbers. Anyway that example helped me understand the two concepts. I have a new understanding on how the two are linked. You cannot measure one without the existence of the other and we should strive to understand the structure of the subjects and events we study.
Leslie Reagan’s, When Abortion was a Crime, was an outstanding example of how to interpret data as presented by understanding the context of the conversations. She learned more by what was not said, than said. For example she understood that for the actual procedure to be referred to in code or to be left unspoken, was an indicator of prevalence. My only issue with that as I so ineloquently stated in class was that the people being interviewed in the inquest already understood the subject matter which might explain why they didn’t completely articulate the subject matter. That in itself caused zero problems for me because of how airtight the remainder of her research was. I hope I can put together a piece of slam dunk history like that one day. Talk to you in a couple of weeks. –Paul.
