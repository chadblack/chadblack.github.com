week 2 - Marxists reading

Posted: Sun, 30 Jan 2011

First, Marxists whether historians,sociologists or philosophers, all talk of the same thing, that is economic classes that exist in the society and how the poor are being oppressed by the elite. In this article Marxist historians try to advocate for social change by contributing to class consciousness and recovering of past experiences. I think as much as historians want to understand what really happenned in the past, we can never really know, we can only get to know or understand a certain portion of the bigger picture. Take for example,trying to understand a culture totally different from your own, even if one tries to socialize with people from that culture one can never really know exactly the ongoings of that society because we are socialized differently. Take myself as an example, an international student from Africa, first time in America, nomatter how hard i will try to understand the American culture i can never really understand all of it. Which takes us back to last week’s discussion truth and accuracy. How much truth is there in historical documents and how much of it is just mere speculation? I believe that the deeper one goes with their research, the less truth there is to it and the more speculation there is. I really dont mean to be too skeptical but historians tend to take a side nomatter how unbiased they try to be,for example, the Marxists never really liked the elites, so obviousy they would take the side fo the “opressed”. My point here is, we as historians really need to be open minded and be able to critically analyze our sources because somewhere somehow they are bound to be biased.

That’s my story and i’m sticking to it…
