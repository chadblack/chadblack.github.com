easter

Posted on April 4th, 2010 by Andrew

Since there isn’t a set topic for this week’s blog, my post will basically be a rant or something of the sort.

First of all, I know this is random but I highly recommend that some of you check out the blog “Civil War Memory” at cwmemory.com . What is so great about this blog (written by a high school history teacher) is that on a regular basis it destroys the popular myth of the “Black Confederate” by examining historical cases emphasized and promoted by modern day Lost Cause “historians.” The blog’s owner and other contributors find that the sources used in these cases are falsified or altered. For instance the wording of a Freed African-American in 1861 describing his two sons “serving in the army” is misinterpreted. Their roles are assumed to be the same as other men in the same unit – fighting soldiers. However the majority of the “Black Confederates” turn out to have been laborers, cooks, or enslaved servants of white soldiers. Even though this is an extreme case, its interesting to read how such myths are constructively destroyed step by step.

I’m also looking forward to reading about Che Guevera’s death even though I have been largely indifferent to the topic of him over the years. Perhaps the cult of Che and the realities of communism has turned me off in the past? Personally my favorite dead commie is Tito of Yugoslavia. Anyways, I’m interested in learning more about the man than the icon but am very skeptical of any writings on him.
