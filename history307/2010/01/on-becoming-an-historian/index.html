On Becoming an Historian

Posted on January 25th, 2010 by Dr. Chad

A few months ago, Inside Higher Ed ran this interview with the co-editors of the recently published book, Becoming Historians. Its an interesting reflection on the on what drew individuals from an important generation of modern scholarship into the profession, and even more specifically, into the methodological and content subdisciplines they helped define. Both the interview and the book are worth a look.

I’m also interested, given our discussion last week and some of your posts in the wake of that discussion, what drew you all to history? Why did you decide to major in History? It’s certainly not a commitment to economic rationality – for which I applaud you at this stage of your lives. I’m not interested in the question, “What are you going to do with your degree,” which goes to an aspect of the “banking model of education.” Kat mentioned in her post for this week that she “fell back” into History after traversing Anthropology and English in part because of the people she’d encountered in history classes– both professors and compañeras. What about the rest of you. Gives us a comment below if want.
