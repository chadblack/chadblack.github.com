Week 1

Posted on January 20th, 2010 by The Raven

Our discussion this week was very enlightening I had never thought about the processes students go through or to what end they served. The banking system, or the financial system, molds students into cookie-cutter models in order to quietly sit and work in the system of American business. I had never thought about the way in which people were taught or that anything was wrong with the “this happened at this time” method of history. Getting really into the idea of thinking why a certain event happened and what effect it had on future events and how the events relate to modern people is far more useful than merely knowing the facts such as, William the Conqueror invaded the British Isles in 1066. The knowledge of this date alone does nothing for an individual unless they find themselves asked for it on an obscure game show. What is important is not that William invaded in 1066 but rather what this invasion led to and what affect it had on England and the politics of the time. The problem solving method of history pushes the student to break out of the machine of society and actually question why it is important to understand the topic which he is attempting to study and what he can add to society beyond the simple paper pushing of cubicle life. I think it is important to look past events from the problem solving angle because the past can help us not only understand what our forefathers have gone through but how the events of the past effect us. Learning about the past in an engaged forum allows the student to not only learn the material but to understand what he has learned and formulate his own point of view aside from merely rehashing what he has been told to memorize. The problem solving method allows people to think for themselves, rather than becoming drones. It engages the students so that there is active participation rather than utter silence. as we saw in class Wednesday, the banking system encourages students to stay quite than rather risking looking dumb by speaking their mind.
