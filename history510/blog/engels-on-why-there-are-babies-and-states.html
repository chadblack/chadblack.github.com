Engels on why there are babies and States...

Posted: Sun, 26 Sep 2010
I do not know why the feminist movement did not latch onto Engels during the Women’s Lib era.  His findings that women were the main power manipulators of ancient times should have been a major attraction.  That men forced themselves to the head for economic reason should only re-enforce women’s sense of disfranchisement, when he claimed the first class oppression as being the female sex by the male. (739)  It would seem that Engels is very down on marriage, at least the monogamous kind.  His belief that only oppressed people can have a truly loving relationship seems more politically created rhetoric.  In his struggle with the capitalist class, Engels appears as if he is trying to place a seed of doubt in the minds of the ruling class by questioning their true loyalty to even their marriage partner.  Perhaps this is where the idea of a pre-nuptial agreement came into being.  He further tries to bring the children into the fray with the idea that their greedy parental units could easily disinherit them because they did not like them.  If I forget, ask me in class about my personal knowledge of this.  Back on point; he does come back to the notion that true sex love is marriage, and it is by nature monogamous.
Engels history of the State is quite insightful.  Why the original association of peoples, the need to protect their territory, the need to organize society and maintain order.  Why taxes are actually necessary â€“ proper use of the revenues gained is an entirely different and very long discussion.
The political power and class control the State provides allude to the change in purpose it has undergone while in operation.  That politics is co-opted by the producers of wealth for the protection of their agenda is to say that the workers are being pushed out of a place that is rightfully theirs.  His closing call for equality, brotherhood, and universal education are a hopeful note on which to end.
