Recession/Panic/Depression = Crisis

Posted: Thu, 23 Sep 2010
Marx’s “Crisis Theory” questioned some of the leading economist of the time, Jean-Baptiste Say, David Ricardo, and James Mill.  He seems to laugh at them with the question of their logic behind the idea that there is no overproduction, or glut, of the market, it’s just that other items were under-produced that would make up for it. I had to read that twice to be sure. He calls their idea “fiction” (460).  And just to make his point clear, he spends the next dozen or so pages covering potential crises and what would actually create a crisis. “No crisis can exist unless sale and purchase are separated from one another and come into conflict…” (454)  Later, he defines money as a means of circulation and a means of payment.  In these exchanges, it would seem that Marx is countering the capitalist’s calming statements about economic crisis with statements that the people should be worried because they couldn’t even afford the shoes and shirts they are making if a failure of credit was to happen.  Hmmm.  How prophetic…  Maybe I’m completely off on all this.  What did you all read in this section?
