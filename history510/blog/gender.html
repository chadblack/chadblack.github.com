Gender

Posted: Sun, 07 Nov 2010

In her article, “Gender: A Useful Category of Historical Analysis,” Joan Scott argues that the term ‘gender’ should be employed more in historical studies than it currently is, and that by not using such a functional term that many historians are selling themselves short.  Gender to Scott should be a new topic defined as a “constitutive element of social relationships based on perceived differences between the sexes” and “a primary way of signifying relationships of power.” (1067) She says it is useful to historians because it is practically an uncharted terrain, and it can be politicized.  By applying the concepts of marital relations to political context, “gender is one of the recurrent references by which political power has been conceived, legitimated, and criticized.” (1073) When you look at her argument it is rather convincing how an examination of political, class, and race of a subject through a gender lens may provide better insight into motivations and ideas of the characters being studied.
