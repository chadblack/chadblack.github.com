Thoughts on Feminism and its place in History

Posted: Mon, 08 Nov 2010

Bennett, Judith M.  History Matter: Patriarchy and the Challenge of Feminism.  Philadelphia: University of Pennsylvania Press, 2006.

Joan Scott, “Gender: A Useful Category of Analysis,” American Historical Review 19.5 (1986): 1053-1075.

Concerning Bennet, I found it curious how the feminist struggle took shape in different countries “especially influenced by socialism in Britain, shaped by Marxism in China, interested in women’s culture in the United States, allied with sociology in Brazil, and fractured by ‘woman’ versus ‘gender’ in Japan.”(pg 4)

Her pluralism to other socio-histories is convincing, race, class and Gender(many other factors of course) history are not at competition to see which has more sway in the plight of societies in history, rather they all play their respective roles in combination.

I enjoy the medievalist within her and how she rightly pointed out that in American culture there is an odd understanding that “old” is inherently bad, and “new” is inherently good.  She explains that this is due to the United States being a young country with no interest in harkening back to before its creation as it would remind them of a “tradition-bound, elitist, European past, as past that has been properly replaced by the dynamism of U.S. democracy and multiculturalism.”(pg 34)

Though I enjoyed her defense of pre-modern and early modern history, her eventual purposes I found conflict with.  Her ultimate goal of rejoining feminist history and feminist activism is an incorrect goal.  That in my view, is abusing history, like a Nationalist would claim a link to the people that just so happened to be in his geographic area 500 years before, and gather that the exploits of those people applied to him in the present.

Concerning Scott, I had very little issue the use of the word Gender as she proposed it, so I tended to agree with what she was saying.
