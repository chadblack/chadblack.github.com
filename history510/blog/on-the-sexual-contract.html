On the Sexual Contract...

Posted: Sat, 13 Nov 2010
I am going to hit a few high points in the book that stood out to me…
Carol Pateman sets out in her book, The Sexual Contract to draw attention to the notion that the famous “social contract,” which even the framers of the Declaration of Independence, the Constitution, and the Bill of Rights had in mind as they created the American trinity of liberty (my words not hers), had a little-acknowledged rider to it; the sexual contract.  She states it better as the male sex-right, and that even though the “social contract is a story of freedom; the sexual contract is a story of subjection.” (2) The social contract masks the fact that it was instituted by men, between men, and for men. This is achieved by dividing society into two spheres – civil and private.  By pushing the women completely into the private sphere, men can dominate the civil sphere, and thus dominate society.  It is further hidden by paternalism, which subsumes the female under the protection of the male, who takes the role as the head of household.  This hides the argument further by using the father to exemplify the state, with the family as its citizens dutifully following – including mom. 
In chapter 3 she lays out the basic contract theory: we are individuals born free and equal. (39)  We then choose to be under the authority of the state.  I seem to recall when a group of ‘states’ didn’t want to be under the authority of the “State” there was a little war.  In 1865, after this little war, the “State” acted like a punishing father spanking his children for disobeying his orders.  Though she didn’t use that analogy to disrupt the theory it does sound good to me.  However, onward we go.  Conjugal power would seem a different way of saying sexual power, but Locke uses conjugal as a natural power men have over women from nature. (51)  Pufendorf follows up with the idea that men have this power to get women to consent to subjection, and this is due to male physical strength being greater than a female’s.  I guess he was closer to the days when clubbing meant whacking a female with a stick and dragging her back to the cave.  Yes, I’m laughing right now at that. I know.  I’m a dork, but that is pretty close to the idea
I want to finish this blog with one final thought.  Pateman wrote this stuff in 1988 – 22 years ago.  In her last two chapters she covers ideas about prostitution, sado-masochism, fetishes, and wonders what’s next.  I wonder if she has any new views about the state of female subjection and patriarchy.  I would like to think we’ve made strides toward evening the divide.  There can be no doubt there will always be a difference between men and women, but sometimes those differences complement each other.  I remember a wedding service I heard once.  The preacher made the statement that when God made Eve he used one of Adams ribs.  He didn’t use a bone from his feet so that she was under him or one from his head so she was over him, but from his side as she was to be beside him and work with him.  And what he couldn’t do, she could and vice versa.
