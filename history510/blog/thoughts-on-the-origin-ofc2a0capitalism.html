Thoughts on The Origin of Capitalism

Posted: Sun, 10 Oct 2010

The Origin of Capitalism is a book written by Ellen Meiksins Wood.  In this book before proposing her own theory of the origin of capitalism she effectively breaks down previous understandings of the origin of Capitalism such as Marxists understand of its origins and what Marxists claimed to be fighting the “commercialization Model.”  One of her major critiques of previous Capitalist origin theories was their assumption that Capitalism was an inevitable end to human history, that all throughout history, Capitalism has been attempting to surface but was held back by social and political constraints.  This idea is the “Commercialization Model.”   As Wood pointed out, in Marxist critiques of the old system, they still held on to this model, thus making their attacks also flawed.

I enjoyed Wood’s attack of euro-centric Capitalism as she quickly pointed out that the only thing that set Western Europe apart was Imperialism, as it gave Europeans the necessary amount of capital to overtake other parts of the world.

Wood proposes that capitalism was not an inevitable outcome out of feudalism, as feudalism produced many outcomes in Europe, such as absolutist France.  Wood proposes that the development of Capitalism in some areas in Western Europe was due to the lack of goods and thus need in their respective markets to expand outside the continent. Once this need was there it was followed by an evolution of the “social property relations that generated market imperatives and capitalist ‘laws of motion,’ which imposed themselves on production.”(pg 77)Wood does also mention that Max Weber’s Protestant Ethic did influence in part the development of Capitalism in Western Europe.

Max Weber’s “The Protestant Ethic and the ‘Spirit’ of Capitalism” attempted to explain capitalism outside of the materialistic terms that Marx set as standard.  I found this admirable.  He promoted the ideas of work as means of glorification of God and self-improvement through work, which got translated into the promotion of Capitalism.
