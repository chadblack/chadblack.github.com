Hegel on the philosophy of history

Posted: Sat, 18 Sep 2010

Hegel’s types of history – original, reflective, philosophical – make sense when one looks at the emerging discipline of history in the European intellectual world.  It actually does what the people and governments wanted from history of the time; to know where they came from, to relate it to their time, and then to ponder the why of things, and where civilization was going.  In the first part Hegel does well explaining the basis for history with Herodotus and Thucydides, and what defines original history.  Breaking down reflective into universal, pragmatical, critical, and fragmentary allows for easier classification of many manuscripts during his day.  I especially liked that he noted the debate about how history ought to be written was frustrating; it appears this question has been around a while. (4)  And he makes a note that writers were not distinguishing their time from the time about which they wrote. (5) His whole Spirit/Reason/Providence section was confusing and daunting. I look forward to hearing comments on that both here and in class.
When I got to the ‘Geographical Basis of History’ part it was plain that Hegel was a man of his times, and very Eurocentric.  It was like he was channeling Buffon claiming the Americas were totally inferior, and as such their history was of no import.  Then, continent by continent he checks them off as having no bearing on “true” world history, which can only come from the Mediterranean area; and Germany of course with Julius Caesar’s crossing of the Alps.  For all his comments about blacks and slavery being a “natural condition,” I like that he comes back to say, “Slavery is in and for itself injustice, for the essence of humanity if Freedom.”  (99) This is just a few observations of mine.  The quick outline I did for this took a page and a half.  What struck the rest of you as odd?  Was anything eye-opening? I found a lot of his work still relevant for today, just not the we’re better than everyone else part.
