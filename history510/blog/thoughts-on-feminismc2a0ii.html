Thoughts on Feminism II

Posted: Tue, 16 Nov 2010

thoughts on The Sexual Contract

I found it enlightening to be informed on contract theory, as a whole.  The victory that social contract theory had over Patriarchy was only due to a rigid, literalist interpretation of Patriarchy, that which views all of the arrangements in a civil society around the Father, however they failed to view what Pateman calls the Sexual contract, the contract between a man and a woman within a society, that still guarantees the man dominance over the woman, another Patriarchy.

According to Pateman the Social contract excludes women and creates a men only society. When she examined the economic opportunities for women, the glass ceiling is keeping women out of the capitalist system, even when women attain entrance into the capitalist system, they never achieve the status of capitalist, only as wage laborers or wage slaves in positions deemed “feminine”.

She compared the slave contract and sexual contract and argued for their horrendous similarity in their one-sidedness, and here is where I take issue with her.  The slave contract has a violence factor that the sexual contract does not have.  I feel like her comparison is deeply exaggerated.  She argued for their similarities in law… but the truth is that a slave had a vastly different experience than that of a wife.  The slave was subject to oppression in ways that the wife could not claim.

thoughts on Gender Trouble

“The task of this inquiry is to center on—and decenter—such defining institutions: phallogocentrism and compulsory heterosexuality.”  (Pg xxix)  What Butler is referring to here is not only questioning the centralization of feminist study on the woman, but more specifically the sexual orientation and its assumption of heterosexuality within the school of Feminism.  Butler is bringing into light queer theory.  I found this more enlightening than something to argue against, it brings to light the failure of assumption.
