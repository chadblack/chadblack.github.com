Meanwhile... back in "The New World"

Posted: Thu, 09 Sep 2010

“How to Write the History of the New World” by Jorge Ca√±izares-Esguerra


It would appear that, while most Spaniards were searching for the little yellow rocks, others were busying themselves by collecting - and subsequently destroying - Amerindian writings on the grounds that they were demonic. And this simply because the images used to describe events looked like demons and snakes and such. Didn’t the European maps of the time also have serpents and sea creatures on them? Or, was this a decided effort by the conquering armies to break the unifying effect of a collective history? The attempts of the indigenous people to hide and protect their writings speak to the importance they gave to these manuscripts. The sad part is that these manuscripts were parceled out later to the highest bidders.
