Why can't these guys just get along?

Posted: Mon, 06 Sep 2010

“How to Write the History of the New World” by Jorge Cañizares-Esguerra:

The question of who had the “correct” interpretation of Mesoamerican history seems the overriding question during the 17th and 18th century in Spain. And while they were at each other’s throats they were united in saying that the rest of Europe didn’t have a clue about what was hidden in the non-Latin based indigenous sources because they weren’t even around while the Spaniards were sacking and pillaging cities and villages in their insatiable lust for gold.  This meant they were not in the country enough time to learn the local ways before the elders were gone. 
As to the in-fighting, I guess it would be hard to write a critical history with the knowledge that if you wrote against the government it could get you exiled, ostracized, censured, or worse. Speaking of the rest of Europe, did anyone on this side of the pond like what Buffon was writing?
Finally, was it me or did all of them eventually make Biblical references and links from and to Mesoamerican historical accounts?
