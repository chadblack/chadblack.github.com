Thoughts on Marx and why I will to my best to Avoid him in the Future…

Posted: Sun, 26 Sep 2010

Marx says, “The relations of production in their totality constitute what is called the social relations, society, and, moreover, a society at a definite stage of historical development, a society with peculiar, distinctive characteristics. Ancient society, feudal society, bourgeois (or capitalist) society, is such totalities of relations of production, each of which denotes a particular stage of development in the history of mankind.”

He is boiling down society in history to mere production of goods or commodities.  Now as a historian in training I will not deny the importance of economy in politics(arguably they are mirror images), a great deal of events throughout history can be attributed to economic standpoints, HOWEVER, the fact that he ignores the affects of culture is simply repulsive.  Culture which in my view is intimately tied with Religion has had great impact in history weighing in as much as economics in the course of time.

Being a Medievalist, I cannot deny that individuals or classes in the medieval period acted for economic gain, however i cannot also deny that they did not believe in the religious righteousness of their actions.  An example would be the Crusades.  European Crusaders fought for the Holy Land for many reasons, Economic, Political and Cultural. Marx’s seemingly economic extreme denies that any individual would do anything without attaining some sort of commodity in return.

Postone explained “the circulation of commodities overcomes the temporal, spatial, and personal barriers imposed by the direct exchange of products.” This might be what Marx said but it’s does not take into account how Culture resisted and denied the circulation of commodities in certain places and times.  China and Japan resisted global markets out of cultural reasons and were only brought to the fold of globalization after invasion or coercion by global powers.  Marx never fully appreciates the power of culture and its imposition on economics itself! He always assumes it goes the other way around.
