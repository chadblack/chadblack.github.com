Thoughts on "That Noble Dream"

Posted: Mon, 18 Oct 2010

That Noble Dream: The “Objectivity Question” and the American Historical Profession by Peter Novick

This to me was a very entertaining book but above that It made me stop and think of my own understanding of history.   It managed to enlighten me on the greater discourse that seems ever present in modern historical writing, that being Objectivity vs Relativity. Two very difficult concepts to define, to my understanding Objectivity is the school of historical thought ever obsessed with obtaining Truth.  ”Truth is one, not perspectival.” Standing against this belief of a Truth within the Past are the relativists who embrace perspective and the multiplicity of relative truths, not simply one.

This dilemma really baffled me.  Where do I fall?  I realize that obtaining “THE Truth” from the past is seemingly impossible but the attempt must not  be abandoned.  When it comes to my understanding of Truth I would more likely fall with the relativists, but concerning my methods I would go with Objectivists as attempting  to undertake an understand of historical interpretations relative the “the historian’s[me] time, place, values, and purposes,” would bog down my work to no end.

Concerning my thoughts on Mark Bevir’s article,  it was a nice complement to the historical profession as he advocated a justification for objective knowledge outside the critiques “the historicity of our being, the influence of power on discourse, [and] the absence of any stable meanings” by applying by applying something very familiar in the historical tool box, Comparison.
