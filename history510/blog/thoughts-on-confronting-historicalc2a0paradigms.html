Thoughts on Confronting Historical Paradigms

Posted: Mon, 04 Oct 2010

This was a heavy read, but the overall message I truly appreciated.  The point of the book was to challenge the sweeping general theories of the Capitalist World System, Labor, and peasantries by using Africa and Latin America as the case examples to how the system doesn’t fit.  I enjoyed how the creation of the world capitalist system was done by historians who did not engage in “issues of anthropology and social science, language and literature, and ‘native’ worlds of knowledge and scholarship.”  The ignoring of  these schools of thought by what the book often refers to as “north atlantic historians” failed to point out that agrarian society vs industrialized society don’t fit Capitalism World System.  Peasantry resistance to Capitalism essentially denied the Capitalist World System with use of flight, banditry, refusal to produce cash crops by returning to subsistence farming and sabotage of cash crops.  When the authors analyze the Latin American and African contexts, the Capitalism World System simply doesn’t fit the complexities of non-western society.
