Schedule and Readings

All readings linked below are for educational use only and are intended for participants in History 510 at the University of Tennessee.

Week 1: Introduction – 8/23

William Cronon, "Getting Ready to Do History,” Carnegie Essays on the Doctorate, Carnegie Initiative on the Doctorate, Carnegie Foundation, Palo Alto, 2004, 1-18.

Recommended general works/broad interpretations:

  • H. Butterfield, The Whig Interpretation of History. New York: Norton, 1965.
  • E. H. Carr, What is History?. New York: Knopf, 1961.
  • Elizabeth A. Clark, History, Theory, Text: Historians and the Linguistic Turn. Cambridge: Harvard Univ. Press, 2004.
  • Sande Cohen, History Out of Joint: Essays on the Use and Abuse of History Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 2006.
  • R. G. Collingwood, The Idea of History. Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1993 [1946].
  • Richard J. Evans, In Defense of History. New York: Norton, 1999.
  • Anna Green and Kathleen Troup, The Houses of History. New York: NYU Press, 1999.
  • Georg G. Iggers, Historiography in the Twentieth Century: From Scientific Objectivity to the Postmodern Challenge. Hanover, NH: Wesleyan Univ. Press, 1997.
  • David Lowenthal, The Past is a Foreign Country. Cambridge: Cambridge Univ. Press, 1985.
  • What is ——— History?, History Today vol. 35, (Jan-June, 1985).

Some journals that are oriented towards theory/philosophy of history:

Clio

Critical Inquiry

History Workshop

Radical History Review

Week 2: The Footnote and Historical Practice – 8/30 [FIRST SELF-REFLECTIVE ESSAY DUE]

  • Anthony Grafton, The Footnote. Cambridge: Harvard Univ. Press, 1997.
Week 3: Labor Day – 9/6 [No class.]

Week 4: Historiographic Sparring in the Early Modern World – 9/13

  • Jorge Canizares-Esguera, How to Write the History of the New World. Stanford: Stanford Univ. Press, 2001.
Week 5: German Idealism – 9/20

  • Immanuel Kant, "Idea for a Universal History from a Cosmopolitan Point of View.”
  • Georg W. F. Hegel, The Philosophy of History (NY: Prometheus, 1991), Introduction, 1-102 (of this section the most important and least obtuse material from 1-11 and 54-79).
  • Leopold von Ranke, The Secret of World History (NY: Fordham U. Press, 1981), 55-72, 101-117, 242-3, 247-254, 258-60.
Week 6: Materialism – 9/27

  • Origins of Family, Private Property, and the State, The German Ideology, "Wage Labor and Capital,” and "Crisis Theory,” Capital Vol. 1, in The Marx-Engels Reader.
  • Moishe Postone, Time, Labor and Social Domination Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1993, Selection.
Week 7: Materialism II – 10/4

  • Steve Stern, et al, Confronting Historical Paradigms. Madison: University of Wisconsin Press, 1994.
Week 8: State and Society – 10/11

  • Max Weber, The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism. New York: Norton, 2009.
  • Ellen Meiskins Wood, The Origin of Capitalism. New York: Verso Books, 2002.
Week 9: The quest for objectivity in the U.S. 10/18

  • Peter Novick, That Noble Dream, Sections I-III. Cambridge: Cambridge Univ. Press, 1988.
  • Mark Bevir, "Objectivity in History,” History and Theory 33, 3 (October 1994).
Week 10: Ethnography and Historical Anthropology – 10/25

  • Clifford Geertz, "Thick Description,” in The Interpretation of Cultures: Selected Essays. NY: Basic Books, 1983.
  • Barber and Berdam, The Emperor’s Mirror. Tucson: Univ. of Arizona Press, 1998.
  • Marshall Sahlins, "Other Times, Other Customs: The Anthropology of History,” American Anthropologist 85,3 (Sept., 1983), 517-544.
Week 11: A Different Cultural Turn – 11/1

  • Michel Foucault, Discipline and Punish: The Birth of the Prison New York: Vintage, 1995.
Week 12: Gender and Sexuality I – 11/8

  • Joan Scott, "Gender: A Useful Category of Analysis,” American Historical Review 19.5 (1986): 1053-1075.
  • Judith Bennett, History Matters. Philadelphia: Univ. of Pennsylvania Press, 2006.
Week 13: Gender and Sexuality II – 11/15

  • Carole Pateman, The Sexual Contract. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 1988.
  • Judith Butler, Gender Trouble: Feminism and the Subversion of Identity. preface, chapters 1 & 3.
Week 14: Theorizing the Archive – 11/22

  • Dan Cohen and Roy Rosenzweig, Digital History: A Guide to Gathering, Preserving, and Presenting the Past on the Web.
  • Antoinette Burton, Archive Stories. Durham: Duke Univ. Press, 2005.
Week 15: Logics of History – 11/29

  • William Sewell, Logics of History: Social Theory and Social Transformations. Chicago: Univ. of Chicago Press, 2005.
